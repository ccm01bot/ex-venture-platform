# AI News: The Last 48 Hours

* **Moonshot AI — Kimi K2.6 goes GA:** Moonshot AI officially released **Kimi K2.6**, a **1-trillion-parameter** mixture-of-experts model with **256K context** and long-horizon coding capabilities. It scores **58.6 on SWE-Bench Pro**, beating GPT-5.4 (57.7) and Claude Opus 4.6 (53.4), and can orchestrate up to **300 sub-agents** across **4,000 coordinated steps**. Available now on Kimi.com, the Kimi App, API, and Kimi Code CLI at **$0.95/M input tokens**. [Source: https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/04/20/moonshot-ai-releases-kimi-k2-6-with-long-horizon-coding-agent-swarm-scaling-to-300-sub-agents-and-4000-coordinated-steps/]

* **Watershed — AI Agents for Sustainability:** **Watershed** launched new AI agents for automated data cleaning and analysis at San Francisco Climate Week. Across test customers, their data cleaning agents have cut **time to actionable sustainability data by 80%**. [Source: https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/04/21/tmt-newswire/globenewswire/watershed-launches-new-ai-agents-and-sustainability-ai-fellowship/2324598]

* **Cerebras — Files for IPO after massive OpenAI deal:** AI chip startup **Cerebras Systems** filed its **S-1** with the SEC on April 17, targeting a **$35 billion valuation**. This follows OpenAI doubling its Cerebras commitment to over **$20 billion** over three years for wafer-scale inference chips, with OpenAI also receiving **equity warrants** in the company. [Source: https://theaiinsider.tech/2026/04/21/cerebras-systems-files-for-ipo-after-23b-valuation-and-openai-deal/]

* **xAI — Grok Speech-to-Text and Text-to-Speech APIs launch:** **xAI** shipped standalone **Grok STT and TTS APIs** on April 17 — the same stack powering Grok Voice, Tesla vehicles, and Starlink support. STT handles **25 languages** with speaker diarization and word-level timestamps at **$0.10/hour batch**. TTS offers **5 expressive voices** across **20 languages** with emotional tags like [laugh] and [sigh] at **$4.20/M characters**. [Source: https://x.ai/news/grok-stt-and-tts-apis]

* **xAI — Grok 4.3 Beta drops for SuperGrok Heavy:** **Grok 4.3 Beta** launched April 17 exclusively for **SuperGrok Heavy** subscribers ($300/month). It adds **native video understanding**, **PDF creation**, **PowerPoint generation**, and **spreadsheet output** while retaining the **2-million-token context window**. Full rollout expected mid-to-late May. [Source: https://dev.to/techsifted/grok-43-review-whats-new-in-xais-latest-model-april-2026-4l2l]

* **Anthropic — Claude Design launches:** On April 17, Anthropic launched **Claude Design**, an Anthropic Labs product powered by **Claude Opus 4.7** that lets teams create **designs, prototypes, slides, and one-pagers** through conversation. It reads your codebase to auto-build a **custom design system** and exports to **Canva, PDF, PPTX, or HTML**. Available for **Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise** subscribers. [Source: https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/17/anthropic-launches-claude-design-a-new-product-for-creating-quick-visuals/]

* **Anthropic — Claude Opus 4.7 ships:** **Claude Opus 4.7** went GA on April 16 as Anthropic's new flagship, delivering stronger **multi-step task performance**, improved **long-horizon reasoning**, and the first Claude model with **high-resolution image support** (up to **2576px / 3.75MP**). It introduces **task budgets** for agentic workflows. Pricing unchanged at **$5/$25 per million tokens** input/output. [Source: https://venturebeat.com/technology/anthropic-releases-claude-opus-4-7-narrowly-retaking-lead-for-most-powerful-generally-available-llm]

* **MiniMax — M2.7 self-evolving agent model open-sourced:** **MiniMax** released **M2.7**, a **229B MoE** open-source model that participated in its own development cycle — running **100+ autonomous improvement rounds**. It scores **56.22% on SWE-Pro** and **57.0% on Terminal Bench 2**. The model now handles **30-50% of daily workflows** inside MiniMax's own RL team. Available on Hugging Face. [Source: https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/04/12/minimax-just-open-sourced-minimax-m2-7-a-self-evolving-agent-model-that-scores-56-22-on-swe-pro-and-57-0-on-terminal-bench-2/]

* **Meta — Muse Spark debuts (proprietary):** **Meta** launched **Muse Spark** on April 8, its first model from **Meta Superintelligence Labs** and first-ever **non-open-weights** release. It's a natively multimodal reasoning model with tool-use, visual chain of thought, and multi-agent orchestration, scoring **52 on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index** — behind only Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT-5.4, and Claude Opus 4.6. Currently in **private API preview**. [Source: https://venturebeat.com/technology/goodbye-llama-meta-launches-new-proprietary-ai-model-muse-spark-first-since]

* **Zhipu AI — GLM-5.1 open-sourced under MIT:** **Z.ai** (formerly Zhipu AI) released **GLM-5.1**, a **754B MoE** model under the **MIT license** that scores **58.4 on SWE-Bench Pro**, beating GPT-5.4 and Claude Opus 4.6 on coding benchmarks. Fully open-weight, commercially usable, built entirely **without American chips**. [Source: https://medium.com/the-ai-studio/glm-5-1-just-beat-gpt-5-4-and-claude-opus-and-its-free-and-open-source-049312ed1836]

* **Google — Gemma 4 open models launch:** **Google DeepMind** released **Gemma 4** on April 2 under the **Apache 2.0 license** — four variants from **2.3B to 31B parameters** with **256K context**, native vision and audio, and **140+ languages**. The 31B model ranks **#3 on Arena AI** at **1452 Elo**, outperforming models 20x its size. Math benchmark jumps from **20.8% to 89.2%** vs Gemma 3. [Source: https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/gemma-4/]

* **Microsoft — Agent Framework 1.0 ships:** **Microsoft** shipped **Agent Framework 1.0 GA** on April 3 — the production-ready convergence of **Semantic Kernel** and **AutoGen** into a single unified SDK. It includes multi-agent orchestration, **A2A + MCP** support, works with any model provider, across **.NET and Python**. Comes with **DevUI**, a browser-based debugger for visualizing agent execution in real time. [Source: https://devblogs.microsoft.com/agent-framework/microsoft-agent-framework-version-1-0/]
